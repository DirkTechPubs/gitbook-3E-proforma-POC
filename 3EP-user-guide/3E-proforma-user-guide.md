# Overview

3E Proforma is an intuitive proforma distribution and mark-up solution for professional services organizations. It optimizes performance and simplifies administrative and proforma tasks using browser-based technology. It provides convenient proforma distribution and editing capabilities for your mobile professionals. It enhances productivity, accuracy, and security. 3E Proforma replicates traditional proforma notations and processes. It frees up your Accounting staff members from cumbersome and time-consuming proforma distribution and mark-up procedures.

## About 3E Proforma

The traditional proforma process is inherently cumbersome and time consuming. By eliminating the reams of paper that must be circulated between the Accounting staff and the timekeepers, and transforming handwritten changes, notations, and explanations to typewritten information, 3E Proforma provides a unique solution that simplifies, expedites, and mobilizes the proforma process. It's the fastest way to route proformas, the best way to ensure that instructions and mark-ups are both readable and implemented accurately, and the most efficient method for processing proformas.

## Standard Proforma Processing Without the Paper Chase

3E Proforma transforms the traditional method of routing and marking up proforma printouts into a paperless process that can be completed either in the office or from a remote location. 3E Proforma mirrors accepted proforma mark-up conventions, eliminating the need for Accounting staff and timekeepers to learn a new system. Users simply log on to view their proformas. Mark-ups and annotations previously made by hand can now be executed online.

## Traditional Proforma Workflow

Traditional proforma distribution can be a time-consuming and error-prone process. It consists of the following steps:

1.  The attorney enters his or her time on a matter.

2.  The biller does a "proforma run" in which all the proformas for each attorney in the firm are printed. A typical proforma run could produce thousands of pages of paper.

3.  The proformas are distributed to the attorneys in the firm. Large stacks of paper are placed on their desks. If the firm has multiple office locations, the distribution process requires a courier.

4.  The attorney reviews each piece of paper for each invoice for the month and marks them up by hand.

5.  The marked-up pages are returned to the biller, who manually enters each change in the system.

6.  The biller generates a bill that is sent to the client.

The traditional process is slow. Timekeepers spend a good percentage of their time on pre-billing. Proforma editing creates a bottleneck for firms. Billing staff need to interpret and re-key the timekeepers' edits.

## 3E Proforma Workflow

In contrast to the traditional process, the 3E Proforma workflow saves time and paper. It consists of the following steps:

1.  Electronic proformas are generated in Elite 3E and a list of proformas is sent to 3E Proforma.

<!-- -->

2.  The attorney makes edits online and submits the changes to the biller.

3.  The biller reviews the changes, looking for items that require manual adjustments, such as dividing cards on the proformas.

## Advantages of 3E Proforma

The advantages of 3E Proforma include:

- Users can collaborate with other timekeepers for review or approval.

- Users can provide basic instructions for transfers, write-ups/downs without needing to learn new terminology.

- Reduces (sometimes eliminates) paper used in the proforma process as well as shipping and overnight delivery charges associated with routing proformas to mobile staffers and branch offices.

- Edited proformas can be returned to accounting immediately, expediting the process of turning proformas into deliverable invoices and improving cash flow.

- Email notification can be sent to a timekeeper when a proforma is sent to them for review.

# Getting Started 

## Accessing 3E Proforma

To start 3E Proforma, do the following:

1.  Open your firm's unique 3E Proforma link in your Chrome or MS Edge browser.

**Note**: If you don't know the link, contact Elite Support or Services for assistance

2.  Enter your network credentials.

3.  Click **Sign in**.

### **Exit 3E Proforma**

Do the following to exit 3E Proforma:

1.  Click **Sign out** on the [<u>3E Proforma Navigation menu</u>](#side-navigation-menu).

**Note**: If the Navigation menu is collapsed, click the Sign out icon.

## Working As Another User

Do the following to work as a different timekeeper:

1.  On the [<u>Global Toolbar</u>](#global-toolbar), you will see your Timekeeper’s name. Click the **Down** arrow next to your name to see other timekeepers that have delegated access to their proformas.  If the list is long, you can use the search field to find the timekeeper instead of scrolling.

2.  To view the proformas available to another timekeeper, select the timekeeper from the drop-down list.

3.  Click **Switch timekeepers** on the confirmation message. Your views will change according to what proformas are associated with the selected timekeeper.

For example, if the logged-in timekeeper (Kathryn Jones) selects Jean Foster from the list, then Kathryn Jones will have access to the proformas that are available to Jean Foster.

**Note**: Delegation is set up in the **User Timekeeper Map** form in 3E. For more information about setting up delegation in the User Timekeeper Map, see the [*User/Role Management*](https://help.elite.com/3e-help-mlf/3Ehelp.htm#Subsystems/Elite 3E Administration Guide/About_User_Role_Management.htm) section in the 3E Administration Guide.

You can conduct a quick search for a proforma by entering specific search criteria (e.g., client name, proforma number, or bill group).

You can sort the list by criteria such as client name, fees, and costs. Additionally, the list can be ordered, based on the sorting criteria, from A - Z or Z to A.

#### Paging

A paging bar at the bottom of the list enables you to move forwards or backwards in a multi-page list of proformas. You specify a specific page to jump to and control how many proformas display per page. On long pages, you have the option to quickly jump back to the top of the page.

### **Lists**

#### Navigating Lists in 3E Proforma

3E Proforma displays proformas and card entries in a list format. A variety of options are available to enable you to control how these list display. List can be displayed in card or grid format. Listed items can be grouped by common criteria. Grid columns can be customized (e.g., moved or removed) to suit the unique needs of the user.

 

#### List View Types

Lists (e.g., proformas and card entries) in 3E Proforma can be viewed using two formats, card view and grid view. The card view groups key information in an easily viewable format. The grid format displays records in a table format with additional sorting and column customization options enabling you to customize how list details are displayed.

##### *Column Settings*

Available column settings include the following:

- **Resize columns** - To change the size of a column, click the border and drag it to the preferred width. The grid is refreshed to display the new width or height. Resizing columns may affect which columns and rows are visible in the grid's display area.

- **Autosize Columns** - Select Autosize This Column from the Grid menu to auto-size a selected column. Click Autosize All Columns to autosize all grid columns.

As you scroll left or right, the selected column remains frozen in that position. Select **Pin Column \> No Pin** to disable this option, or select **Reset Columns** .

The Proforma Details view is used to view proforma header information and complete tasks such as reviewing and editing time, cost, and charge cards.

**Note**: Certain workflow actions such as Mark as Complete, Approve, or Submit will also close the Proforma Detail view and return a user to the Proforma List. The following information only applies to non-workflow actions used to exit this view.

- **Save & Close** - The Save & close button saves all edits made to the proforma, recalculates proforma totals and sends the user back to the list of proformas from which he opened the proforma details. The proforma is unlocked and becomes available to other users.

- **Close** - The owner clicks Close to exit the proforma without saving any changes and remove it from the workflow.

- **Cancel** - Click Cancel to unlock the proforma and cancel all unsaved changes. The proforma details page is closed, and the user is back to the proforma list from which proforma details were opened.

### **Divide Entries**

#### About Divide Cards

After time and cost entries have been included in a proforma, the worked and billed hours on the original entry can be divided. The purpose of dividing time and/or cost entries is to exclude them from the proforma, or to partially purge a record.

Calculated charge entries, i.e., taxes, interest, BOA, flat fees, markups, etc., cannot be divided; however, charge entries entered directly, i.e., as anticipated time/costs, etc, can be divided in the same manner as time and cost entries. One or multiple entries can be created for the divided amounts. The original entry is changed to the new reduced value. In addition, the divided entries can be optionally transferred to another proforma.

1.  Locate the proforma containing funds on the Proforma list.

2.  Click the proforma number to access the Proforma Details view.

3.  Click the appropriate tab (i.e., Fees, Costs, or Charge).

4.  Click the card-level **Action** menu for a listed card and select **Divide**.

## Managing Funds

### **Apply Funds**

If the Matter record has funds in Lawyer’s Trust account(s), also known as IOLA or IOLTA bank account(s), funds in Unallocated Billed on Account (BOA), or Unallocated Credits, a Funds tab will display in the proforma details. The Funds tab has sub-tabs for each type of funds that are available to be applied to the proforma, trust and unallocated credits. For example, if the matter has funds in a Lawyer’s Trust account and Unallocated Credits there will be two (2) sub-tabs on the Funds tab. If the Matter record has more than one bank account holding trust funds, there will be a record on the Trust sub-tab for each bank account.

Do the following to apply funds:

1.  Locate the proforma containing funds on the Proforma list.

2.  Click the proforma number to access the Proforma Details view.

3.  Click on the desired sub-tab (i.e., Trust or Unallocated credit).

4.  Expand the row for the available amount to apply to the proforma when the invoice is generated.

5.  Complete the required fields of information. Required fields are designated with an \* next to the field label. See "Proforma Details Form and Field Definitions" on page [46](#proforma-details-form-and-field-definitions) for details.

6.  Click **Apply** to save and apply the funds. An *Applied* badge displays on the funds row. Additionally, the *Available* count decreases by one (1) count and the *Applied* count increases by one (1) on the sub-tab.

 

### **Remove Applied Funds**

Do the following to un-apply trust or credit funds:

1.  Locate the proforma containing funds on the Proforma list.

2.  Click the proforma number to access the Proforma Details view.

3.  Click on the desired sub-tab (i.e., Trust or Unallocated credit).

4.  Click the **X** on the *Applied* badge. the “Available” count increases by one (1) and the “Applied” count decreases by one (1) on the sub-tab.

## Adjustments

### **About Adjustments**

Adjustment cards are used to adjust the amount of the entire proforma total by an amount or a percent. For example, if I want to give my client a 10% discount on fees on a specific Proforma to reduce the overall bill total and show as a courtesy discount on the bill to my client, I will add a percent adjustment card and choose Adjustment type “Courtesy discount”.

To adjust the amount of the proforma down, the adjustment card amount or percentage must include a minus sign (-) upon entry.

An adjustment card can also be entered to target a specific timekeeper’s cards, either all cards for the specified timekeeper or card up to or between specified dates. Or the adjustment can target all timekeepers cards up to or between specified dates. Targeting a timekeeper and dates of cards is not required to add an adjustment to the proforma, these are options.

 

### **Add Adjustments**

Do the following to add an adjustment card:

1.  Access the Proforma Detail view for a proforma.

2.  Click the **Adjustment** tab and click **Add Adjustment**.

**Note**: You can also select Add Adjustment from the Proforma Detail's Action menu.

3.  Select an adjustment **Type** and **Method**.

4.  Select additional adjustment options as needed. See "Add Adjustment Form and Field Definitions" on page [99](#add-adjustment-form-and-field-definitions) for further details.

5.  Click **Save Adjustments** to apply your settings and close the Add Adjustment form.

# Collaboration

## About Collaborator List

3E Proforma provides the ability to create Collaboration groups. These are essentially the workgroup for a proforma, at the matter level. The collaboration group consists of all timekeepers who have time cards, cost cards, or charge cards on the proforma, or who have been added to the collaboration group by the owner of the proforma.

 

**Points to remember:**

- All proforma collaborators can work on any unlocked proforma at any time.

- Only one timekeeper can be the owner of a proforma, unless a [<u>co-owner is assigned to the proforma</u>](#assign-co-owner-to-proforma).

- Timekeeper’s rights cannot be edited in the Collaboration window.

- Timekeepers who have a proforma in the [<u>Proforma List</u>](#navigating-3e-proforma---walkthrough) can see the list of collaborators.

- Only the Billing attorney (proforma owner) can add a new collaborator.

- A new collaborator has full rights to the proforma but can only perform the **Mark as Complete** action as the working timekeeper.

- An automatic email notification can be sent to proforma owners when a collaborator has completed their edits. This ensures that the owner has timely notification that the collaborator edits are finished, and the owner can perform a final review and submit the proforma.

**Note**: To enable the email notification, the 3E Override/Set System Options **Notify_EmailOnComplete** must be set to **True**. It is set to False (i.e., disabled), by default. This feature requires 3E 5.6.0.

## Manage Proforma Collaborators

Do the following to manage the list of timekeepers collaborating on a proforma:

1.  In the Proforma list, click the **Action** menu for a listed proforma and select **View Collaborators**.

**Note**: You can also access the Collaborator list in the Proforma Details view by clicking the Proforma-level **Action** menu and selecting **View collaborators**.

2.  Do one of the following:

- **Add a collaborator** - Click **Add Collaborator** to add another timekeeper to the list.

- **Remove a collaborator** - Click the **Delete** icon <img src="./3EP-UG-img/media/image113.png" style="width:0.13542in;height:0.13542in" /> at the end of the row of a listed collaborator to delete them.

- **Remove multiple collaborators** - Click to remove all collaborators with Edit rights. This action does not affect the Owner and Co-Owner.

3.  Click **Save** to save any changes made to the list of collaborators on the proforma.

 

## Assign Co-owner to Proforma

Proforma owners have the ability to assign a co-owner. The co-owner has the same rights as the proforma owner, allowing for shared proforma responsibilities. Assigning a co-owner to a proforma requires the following steps:

- Designate a co-owner in 3E (i.e., at the matter, client, timekeeper, or proforma level)

- Assign a [<u>collaborator</u>](#collaboration) as a co-owner in 3E Proforma

- Modify 3E Proforma stock workflow configurations in 3E

Once these steps have been completed, both the owner and the co-owner will receive the proforma and have owner rights to edit and submit the proforma in 3E Proforma.

### **STEP 1: Designate a Co-owner in 3E Cloud**

A default co-owner can be set in multiple record types in 3E. It can be set in the *Effective Dated Information* child form of the Client, Matter, and Timekeeper records. Additionally, it can be set in Proforma Generation.

The order of precedence for the default co-owner setting is as follows:

- Proforma Generation

- Matter

- Client

- Timekeeper

**Note**: Assigning a co-owner in Proforma Generation will override a co-owner selected in Client, Matter, and Timekeeper records.

Do one of the following to designate a default co-owner in 3E:

- **Matter, Client, or Timekeeper process**: Navigate to the **Effective Dated Information** child form of either the Matter, Client, or Timekeeper process and specify the user in the **3E Proforma Co-owner** field.

<img src="./3EP-UG-img/media/image114.png" style="width:6.15625in;height:2.98958in" />

- **Proforma Generation**: Go to the Proforma Generation process and specify the user in the **Assign Co-owner** field.

### **STEP 2: Assign Co-owner Rights in 3E Proforma**

Within 3E Proforma, co-owner rights can be assigned to a proforma collaborator.

**Note**: There can only be one Co-Owner assigned to a proforma.

Do the following to give a collaborator co-owner rights for a proforma:

1.  In the Proforma list, click the **Action** menu for a listed proforma and select **View Collaborators**.

**Note**: You can also access the Collaborator list in the Proforma Details view by clicking the Proforma-level **Action** menu and selecting **View collaborators**.

2.  In the Rights column, select **Co-Owner** for a listed collaborator.

<!-- -->

3.  Click **Save**.

<img src="./3EP-UG-img/media/image115.png" style="width:6.15625in;height:2.32292in" />

### **STEP 3: Update 3E Proforma Workflow Steps in 3E Cloud**

To route the proformas to both the owner and co-owner, the following 3E Proforma workflow steps (i.e., rule sets) in the workflow configuration must be updated to use the co-Owner workflow dynamic routing filters:

- **Start Workflow** - dynamic route, collaboration dynamic route

- **Bill Submitted no approval, tier 1, tier 2 rule sets** - email route

- **Biller Retur**n - dynamic route

- **Bill write down approval** - email route

- **Reject proforma** - dynamic route, email route

- **Return proforma** - dynamic route, email route

- **Biller Submitted no approval, tier 1, tier 2** - email route

- **Start workflow from group** - dynamic route (Note, this step handles group proforma workflow, no other group proforma workflow updates needed.)

 

Do the following to update the workflow steps listed above:

1.  In 3E, access the **Workflow Configuration** process.

2.  Select the stock **3E Proforma** workflow configuration.

3.  Select a step (e.g., Start Workflow) and scroll to the **Routing for Next Workflow Setup** section.

4.  Do the following to update the dynamic routing filters for each of the above steps:

<!-- -->

1.  In the field adjacent to the Dynamic Route check box, choose the routing that corresponds to who the Proforma Owner is:

- **Billing Timekeeper** - 3E Proforma – Owner and CoOwner (BillTkpr)

- **Responsible Timekeeper** - 3E Proforma – Owner and CoOwner (RspTkpr)

- **Supervisor Timekeeper** - 3E Proforma – Owner and CoOwner (SpvTkpr)

2.  For the Start Workflow step, in the **Routing for Next Workflow Step** section, choose **3E Proforma – Owner, Co-Owner, and Editor** for the Collaboration \> Dynamic Route.

<img src="./3EP-UG-img/media/image116.png" style="width:6.15625in;height:3.05208in" />

3.  For rules sets that require email routing (e.g., Bill write down approval), click the step's **Workflow Notifications** child form tab.

4.  In the **Dynamic address** column choose the routing that corresponds to who the Proforma Owner (e.g., Billing Timekeeper).

# Archived Proformas

## About Archived Proformas

The Archived Proformas List view displays all proformas that have been archived in the current and prior periods. Proformas automatically move to the Archived Proformas tab in the following cases:

- When the proforma is invoiced in 3E

- When the proforma is Deferred and the days to archive the proforma have passed. The date when the deferred proforma moves to the Archived Proformas tab is calculated as the proforma generation date plus the number of days to archive the deferred proforma.

- When the proforma status is changed to Closed.

The Archived list displays the 3E Invoice number and Invoice Date associated with the proforma.

Actions available with Archived Proformas include:

- Edit History

- Bill Preview

- Print Proforma

 

## Browse Archived Proformas

Do the following to view a list of archived proformas:

1.  Select **Archived Proformas** in the side Navigation menu.

<!-- -->

2.  Specify filtering criteria in the Filter panel to narrow the list of displayed proformas and click **Apply**.

3.  Do one of the following:

- **Batch Action** - You can generate a **Bill Preview** or **Print Proforma** for a batch of selected archived proforma.

- **Single Action** - Click the proforma number for a listed proforma to access the Proforma Details view for an archived proforma. Select actions such as **Edit History**, **Bill Preview**, or **Print Proforma** from the Proforma-level **Action** menu.

# Appendix A - Proforma Action Availability by Role

The proformas that display in the [<u>Proforma list</u>](#navigating-3e-proforma---walkthrough) depend on which [<u>Proforma category</u>](#navigating-3e-proforma---walkthrough) is selected. The availability of [<u>actions</u>](file:///C:/Madcap Flare Projects/3E Proforma Help v2/Content/3E Proforma/Proforma Actions.htm) that can be performed on a proforma is based on the 3E User/Role security settings.

The actions that can be performed in the Proformas List depend on the role of the current user for the exact proforma:

- [<u>Timekeeper</u>](#timekeepers-proforma-editors)

- [<u>Billing Attorney</u>](#billing-attorneys-proforma-owners)

- [<u>Approver</u>](#approvers)

Some proformas in a category have both a status and a sub-status.

<img src="./3EP-UG-img/media/image126.png" style="width:6.5in;height:2.13542in" />

In the example above, the proforma’s status is **Completed**, and the sub-status is **Biller Review**.

This section describes the categories, statuses, sub-statuses, and actions available to users according to the roles of the current user.

## Timekeepers (Proforma Editors)

This section describes the Proforma List options for the timekeeper.

### **My proformas**

Selecting the My proformas category displays all proformas for which the user has a time card or a cost card and which are not marked as completed, submitted, or deferred.

 

Proformas with the following statuses are available:

| **Status** | **Description** |
|----|----|
| **Needs review** | Proformas in this status are waiting for the timekeeper’s review. |
| **In review** | Proformas in this status were opened and/or edited by this timekeeper. |
| **Urgent** | Proformas in this status have met or passed their urgency date. |

 

The following actions are available:

| **Action** | **Description** |
|----|----|
| **Set priority** | Select this action to set a proforma as priority. Other users who have the proforma in their list will also see that it is marked as priority. |
| **Mark as complete** | Select this action to indicate that all the edits for this proforma have been completed. |
| **Forward** | Select this action to forward a proforma to another user. |
| **Add comments** | Select this action to browse/add proforma level comments. |
| **View Collaborators** | Select the action to browse all proforma collaborators |

### **Needs review**

Selecting the **Needs review** list displays all proformas for which the user has a time card or a cost card and the proformas on which the user has been added to the collaboration group but which have not been touched yet. Only proformas in “Needs review” status are shown in the list.

The same actions as in the My proformas list are available.

### **In review**

Selecting the **In review** list displays all proformas that were opened and/or edited by the timekeeper. Only proformas in “In review” status are shown in the list. The same actions as in the **My proformas** list are available.

### **Urgent**

Selecting the **Urgent** list displays all proformas that have met or surpassed their urgency date. Only proformas in “Urgent” status are shown in the category. The same actions as in the **My proformas** list are available.

**Note:** Urgency date is calculated based on a setting in 3E Override / Set System Options and the proforma date.

### **Completed Proformas**

Selecting the **Completed proformas** category displays all proformas that have been completed by a timekeeper. Only proformas in “Completed” status are available in the list. There are no available actions.

# Glossary

A

Approvals

Proformas that need to be approved before going to the Billing Department display in the Approvals list. For example, the workflow might be set up so that all proformas with an amount of \$20,000 or more must be approved by a certain Timekeeper.

Archived Proformas

Proformas that are no longer current because they have either been billed or replaced by another proforma display in the Archived Proformas list.

C

Collaboration Group

The Collaboration Group consists of anyone who has a time card, cost card, or charge card on a proforma, by default. However, the Billing Attorney can add other people to the Collaboration Group.

D

Defer

To defer a proforma is to indicate that you do not want to bill the proforma until a later time.

O

Owner

The lead attorney assigned to the matter, also known as the Billing Attorney. When a proforma is generated in 3E, it is automatically assigned to the Billing Attorney.

P

Proforma

A proforma contains the information required to bill a client for a matter, typically for one month's work. It includes information about the matter (client name and address, matter name, matter description, etc.), time spent on the matter, the rate charged for that time, costs associated with the matter, and instructions for the Billing department. Proformas are edited by timekeepers and then sent to the Billing department to create the bill.

R

Reclaim

To remove a proforma from the Submitted Proformas list and move it back to the Needs review list.

S

Submit

To send a proforma from a Timekeeper to the Billing department.

T

Timekeeper

Anyone who performs work on a matter that can be billed to the client.

W

WIP

Work-in-progress

Write Off

To reduce an amount owed by a client on a proforma by a specified amount.

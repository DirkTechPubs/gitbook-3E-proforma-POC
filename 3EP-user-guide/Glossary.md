# Glossary

A

Approvals

Proformas that need to be approved before going to the Billing Department display in the Approvals list. For example, the workflow might be set up so that all proformas with an amount of \$20,000 or more must be approved by a certain Timekeeper.

Archived Proformas

Proformas that are no longer current because they have either been billed or replaced by another proforma display in the Archived Proformas list.

C

Collaboration Group

The Collaboration Group consists of anyone who has a time card, cost card, or charge card on a proforma, by default. However, the Billing Attorney can add other people to the Collaboration Group.

D

Defer

To defer a proforma is to indicate that you do not want to bill the proforma until a later time.

O

Owner

The lead attorney assigned to the matter, also known as the Billing Attorney. When a proforma is generated in 3E, it is automatically assigned to the Billing Attorney.

P

Proforma

A proforma contains the information required to bill a client for a matter, typically for one month's work. It includes information about the matter (client name and address, matter name, matter description, etc.), time spent on the matter, the rate charged for that time, costs associated with the matter, and instructions for the Billing department. Proformas are edited by timekeepers and then sent to the Billing department to create the bill.

R

Reclaim

To remove a proforma from the Submitted Proformas list and move it back to the Needs review list.

S

Submit

To send a proforma from a Timekeeper to the Billing department.

T

Timekeeper

Anyone who performs work on a matter that can be billed to the client.

W

WIP

Work-in-progress

Write Off

To reduce an amount owed by a client on a proforma by a specified amount.

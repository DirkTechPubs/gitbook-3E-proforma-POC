## Traditional Proforma Workflow

Traditional proforma distribution can be a time-consuming and error-prone process. It consists of the following steps:

1.  The attorney enters his or her time on a matter.

2.  The biller does a "proforma run" in which all the proformas for each attorney in the firm are printed. A typical proforma run could produce thousands of pages of paper.

3.  The proformas are distributed to the attorneys in the firm. Large stacks of paper are placed on their desks. If the firm has multiple office locations, the distribution process requires a courier.

4.  The attorney reviews each piece of paper for each invoice for the month and marks them up by hand.

5.  The marked-up pages are returned to the biller, who manually enters each change in the system.

6.  The biller generates a bill that is sent to the client.

The traditional process is slow. Timekeepers spend a good percentage of their time on pre-billing. Proforma editing creates a bottleneck for firms. Billing staff need to interpret and re-key the timekeepers' edits.
